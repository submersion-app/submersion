# 🎉 Submersion v1.5.7.110 Release Notes

v1.5.7 builds on the bulk-editing and Garmin work from v1.5.6. A dive can now carry more than one dive type at once (ex. a single dive can be recreational and shore). It also makes your air-consumption numbers more accurate by accounting for real-gas compressibility, finishes Garmin FIT import by recovering cylinder size, makes the dive-profile chart noticeably faster to draw, and clears a stack of reported issues. Most importantly, it eliminates a multi-second stutter with flickering detail cards during sync, stalled OSTC nano and Android serial downloads, and an OTU statistic that counted dives it shouldn't have. Under the hood, the app moves to Flutter 3.44.

---

## ✨ New and improved

### Multiple dive types per dive

Until now a dive had exactly one dive type, chosen from a single dropdown. But dive categories aren't mutually exclusive — a dive can be recreational, shore, and photography all at the same time. A dive can now carry as many types as fit, consistently across the whole app:

- **Multi-select in the editor:** the dive-type field shows your selected types as chips at rest and expands to a checkbox list so you can tick several.
- **Everywhere it matters:** all of a dive's types appear on the dive detail screen and in a dive-table column.
- **Filter by membership:** filtering the dive list by a type now matches every dive that *carries* that type, not just dives whose single type was an exact match.
- **Statistics count each type:** a multi-type dive counts toward every one of its types, so per-type counts can add up to more than your total dive count — expected for multi-label data.
- **Bulk editing:** change dive types across a whole selection with **Add / Remove / Replace** and one-tap undo, in the same collection lane as tags, buddies, gear, and weights.
- **Import/export:** multiple types survive a round-trip through UDDF, and they appear in CSV, Excel, and PDF exports.

Existing dives keep their current type, and the change is backward-compatible over sync — older app versions still read a single "representative" type and keep working. Every dive still has at least one type, so there's no new "untyped" state to deal with.

### More accurate air consumption (gas compressibility)

Your SAC/RMV rate is computed from how much gas leaves your tank. The previous math treated gas as ideal, but real gases don't compress perfectly linearly, the effect is largest at the high pressures in a full tank and with helium mixes. SAC rate and the statistics built on it now apply a compressibility (Z) factor and use the correct bar-to-atmosphere conversion, so consumption figures are more accurate, especially for high-pressure fills and trimix. Thanks to community contributor **essengee** for this improvement.

### Garmin cylinder size now imports

Building on v1.5.6's full-fidelity Garmin FIT import: a Garmin `.fit` file doesn't actually store your cylinder size, but it does record how much gas you used — a number Garmin computes *from* the size you configured. The importer now reverses that calculation to recover each air-integration tank's cylinder volume (rounded to 0.1 L), so imported Garmin dives arrive with tank size already filled in and feed SAC correctly, with no manual entry. When the derivation isn't reliable, the tank still imports with its pressures and gas, just without a volume.

### Faster dive-profile chart

The dive-profile chart now caches its rendered series, and combining pressure data from multiple tanks was reworked to scale linearly instead of quadratically. Long dives and multi-tank dives draw noticeably faster, and the chart does less work when you pan, zoom, or hover. (Further groundwork for very long profiles continues behind the scenes.)

### Snappier launch

The splash-screen fade-out was trimmed to dissolve in 750 ms, so the app feels a little quicker on startup.

## 🐞 Bug fixes

### Sync no longer stutters, and dive cards stop flickering

On libraries syncing a lot of changes, the app could stutter for 20–30 seconds while the deco, tissue-loading, O2, and SAC-by-segment cards on the dive detail screen flickered as data streamed in. Database change notifications are now coalesced into a single update, and the detail cards hold their last-good content through a transient empty moment instead of blanking out. A related crash on the dive detail screen, introduced by the Riverpod 3 upgrade's automatic stream pausing, is fixed as well.

### OSTC nano Bluetooth downloads complete

This is the second half of the OSTC nano fix from v1.5.5/v1.5.6. Some OSTC nano firmware reports a longer dive profile than it actually sends, so the download kept waiting for bytes that never arrived and failed. The download now reads adaptively and stops at the true end-of-profile marker, so these dives transfer completely. (A known edge case — telling a profile that lost data mid-transfer apart from a genuinely complete one — is documented for a future pass.)

### Serial dive-computer downloads on Android

On Android release builds, downloading from a serial/USB dive computer (reported with a Mares Puck Pro) could fail because the release optimizer renamed methods that the USB-serial driver looks up by name. Those methods are now protected from renaming, so serial downloads work on shipping Android builds. A build-time check guards against the rule being dropped again.

### Weekly OTU no longer counts later dives

The rolling weekly OTU (pulmonary oxygen-exposure) total shown for a dive was including dives logged *after* it, inflating the figure. It now counts only that dive plus the prior seven days.

### Bulk-edit banner can be dismissed

The banner shown while bulk-editing dives now auto-hides and can be dismissed, so it no longer lingers on screen.

### Dive Sites filter sheet renders correctly

After the framework update, the Dive Sites filter sheet could appear without its proper background and text styling. It now renders correctly.

### Settings navigation "move down" fixed

In Settings, reordering the bottom navigation with the move-down button had an off-by-one after a framework API change. Items move where you expect again.
