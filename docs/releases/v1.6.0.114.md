# 🎉 Submersion v1.6.0.114 Release Notes

v1.6.0's centerpiece is a completely rebuilt dive planner: a live profile canvas that recomputes your schedule as you edit, real closed-circuit planning with a worst-case bailout solver, contingency and lost-gas plans, turn pressure and rock-bottom checks, and plans that save, sync, share, and print. Around it, this release adds three new ways to get dives into your logbook: scan a paper logbook page with your camera, import a whole folder of dive files at once, and record a surface GPS track on your phone that positions dives your computer couldn't. It also gives you a way out of a stuck sync, unifies the cylinder information on the dive detail page into one card, and fixes the reason large Shearwater logbooks could never finish downloading.

---

## ✨ New and improved

### A completely rebuilt dive planner

The old three-tab form planner is gone. In its place is a **Live Profile Canvas**: you edit only the bottom of your dive, and the ascent, the deco stops, the gas plan, and every number on screen recompute as you type. There is no Calculate button.

- **The chart is the plan.** Your profile draws live with a dashed ceiling overlay and gas-switch markers. Drag across it to scrub, and a readout follows your finger with runtime, depth, ceiling, and time-to-surface at that instant. Status chips above the editor keep runtime, NDL (which flips to TTS the moment you incur deco), deco time, CNS, and a tappable count of any problems with the plan always in view. On phones the results live in a swipe-up sheet; on desktop they get their own always-visible pane beside the chart.
- **Results that read like a dive slate.** The results sheet carries the deco schedule as a runtime table (air breaks included), per-cylinder gas consumption with remaining pressure that turns red when you break your reserve, turn pressure and minimum gas per bottom cylinder, and a severity-sorted list of warnings. A plan that can't be dived says so rather than failing.
- **Closed-circuit planning.** Flip the OC/CCR chip and set your low and high setpoints and switch depth. Rebreather plans now compute real loop decompression at your setpoints rather than pretending you're on open circuit, route metabolic oxygen and diluent to the right cylinders, and warn when a deco plan carries no bailout. A **bailout solver** runs a full open-circuit ascent from many points along your bottom and reports the worst case: the runtime and depth it happens at, the bailout TTS, and the gas you'd need against the gas you're actually carrying. Scrub the chart in CCR mode and the readout shows your bailout TTS at that instant too.
- **Contingencies.** Chips under the chart switch between your base plan, five metres deeper, five minutes longer, or both, drawing the contingency as a grey ghost profile over your live one. Lost-gas plans recompute the schedule for the loss of each deco or stage cylinder in turn. Turn pressure supports all-usable, halves, thirds, or a fraction you choose, and a rock-bottom calculation models a stressed two-diver ascent from your maximum depth, flagging any cylinder that would finish below its minimum gas.
- **It knows your logbook.** "Follow a dive" picks one of your recent dives and seeds the plan's starting tissues from where that dive actually left you, off-gassing across the surface interval, so a repetitive dive plans from your real loading rather than from clean tissues. One tap fills your bottom SAC from your own logged back-gas average. When the dive is done, "Convert to dive" turns the plan into a real logged dive, and from then on the dive's profile chart automatically ghosts the plan behind what you actually did.
- **Take the plan with you.** Save plans and they sync to your other devices like everything else. Print or share a **dive slate PDF** with the runtime table, gas plan, contingencies, and range grid on one high-contrast page. Share a plan as a versioned `.subplan` file that any Submersion install can import (ids are regenerated, so an imported plan never collides with yours). A **range table** re-runs the schedule across a grid of depths and times either side of your plan so you can see the cost of going a little deeper or staying a little longer at a glance. And you can select two or three saved plans and **compare** them, profiles overlaid and differences tabulated.
- **The Planning hub now leads with the planner.** A New Plan button and your three most recent plans sit at the top, and the calculators have moved into a Tools section beneath them. The decompression calculator gained altitude and water-type inputs, so it now agrees with the planner when you're diving at altitude or in fresh water.

Submersion's planner models Bühlmann ZH-L16C with gradient factors. It is a technical planner rather than a recreational one, and as the app itself says, these tools are for planning purposes only: always verify the numbers and dive within your training.

### Scan a paper logbook page with your camera

"Scan Paper Log" in the add-dive sheet photographs a page of a paper logbook, reads it on-device, and opens a new dive with the fields already filled in. It picks up dive number, date, entry time, bottom time, maximum depth, water and air temperature, start and end pressure, oxygen percentage, weight, rating, and the site and location names, and it works out whether the page is in metric or imperial from the units written on it, falling back to your own settings when the page gives no clue. A site name that confidently matches one of your sites is linked for you. Anything it recognizes but has nowhere to put (visibility, your buddy, the divemaster, a site name it couldn't match) is appended to the dive's notes rather than dropped. The photo itself is attached to the dive it created.

Text recognition runs entirely on your device and your photos never leave it: Apple's Vision framework on iPhone, iPad, and Mac, ML Kit on Android, and Windows' built-in recognizer. On Linux it uses Tesseract, which you'll need to install yourself (`sudo apt install tesseract-ocr`); the app tells you so rather than failing quietly.

Treat it as a fast way to capture the occasional page rather than a bulk back-catalog migration. Values that come out physically implausible are left blank instead of guessed, because a misread depth looks exactly as reasonable as a correct one. Tick-boxes and smiley-face rating scales aren't read, cylinder volume isn't extracted, and handwriting on the Linux path in particular is weak. If a page reads poorly, you get a blank dive form with the photo attached rather than a dead end.

### Import a whole folder of dive files at once

Importing dive files no longer means one file at a time. Select as many as you like, or on desktop point Submersion at a folder and let it scan recursively, and you get one review and one summary for the entire batch. Dropping several files, or a folder, onto the app works too. It handles `.fit`, `.uddf`, `.xml`, Subsurface `.ssrf`, and MacDive `.db`/`.sqlite` files; CSV files still need their own field mapping, so they're set aside for individual import rather than blocking the batch, as are unreadable or unrecognized files.

A triage step shows you what was found in each file before anything is parsed. Every dive in the review names the file it came from, and the summary breaks the result down file by file. Dives that duplicate each other *across* files in the same batch are caught before anything is written and default to being skipped. That is the case that bites hardest when you're importing a back catalog for the first time. Sites, buddies, trips, and equipment that appear in several files are folded together by name so a shared site is created once rather than once per file. Cancelling stops cleanly at the next file boundary, and a single corrupt file is reported and skipped instead of taking the batch down with it.

### Record a surface GPS track with your phone

Most dive computers have no GPS, but the phone in your dry bag does. The new **GPS Logger** records a timestamped surface track while you dive, then positions your dives from it. Start it from the dashboard's quick actions or its own GPS Log page, and a slim recording strip stays visible across the whole app so you never forget it's running.

When you later download or import dives, Submersion matches each dive's start and end times against your recorded tracks and stamps entry and exit coordinates onto any dive that doesn't already have them, interpolating between fixes. It runs automatically after a download, after a file import, after a sync merge (so a track recorded on your phone can position a dive you imported on your Mac), and whenever you stop a recording. Positioned dives flow straight into the existing site-matching review, and coordinates that came from your computer or from you are never overwritten.

Recording works on iPhone, iPad, and Android and continues with the app backgrounded or the screen locked; Android shows a persistent notification while it runs and iOS needs only "While Using the App" permission. Finished tracks sync everywhere, so your Mac or PC can browse them and run the matching even though it can't record. It's built for a real boat day: bad fixes are discarded, a moored boat still logs a point every few minutes so a stationary hour doesn't become a hole, and the track is checkpointed as it goes, so a crash costs you minutes rather than the day. Reopen the logger and it recovers the interrupted recording and tells you it did. Recording is manual start and stop for now; automatic starts, drawing tracks on the map, and GPX export aren't in this release.

### The site picker knows where the dive was

When you pick a site for a dive that already carries GPS, the picker now measures from the dive's own coordinates rather than from wherever your phone happens to be. Sites sort nearest-first with their real distance from that dive shown in your units, scaling from metres to kilometres (or feet to miles) as appropriate, and the header confirms it's sorting by distance from this dive. Tapping New Dive Site opens the site form already carrying the dive's coordinates, filling in country and region from a reverse geocode where those fields are empty, so creating a site from a computer-recorded position no longer means retyping a latitude and longitude.

### One Cylinders card instead of two

The dive detail page's Tanks card and its separate SAC-by-Cylinder block have merged into a single **Cylinders** card. Each cylinder reads as one row: name and gas mix, a volume or preset chip, start and end pressure with the amount used, its MOD and MND, and its own SAC rate with the gas consumed. Per-cylinder SAC used to appear only on multi-tank dives and only if you went looking for it inside the SAC-by-segment section; now it shows on any dive where it can be calculated. Your card ordering and visibility settings carry over untouched.

### Estimated tank pressure on manually logged dives

Enter a start and end pressure for a cylinder with no transmitter data and the profile chart now draws that pressure as a straight interpolated line rather than nothing at all. It's drawn straight rather than curved so it's never mistaken for a real transmitter trace, and the legend and tooltip both label it "(est.)". It understands your gas switches: the line holds flat while a cylinder isn't being breathed and falls only across the windows where it is, so a deco bottle reads flat-drop-flat and a back gas you return to reads drop-flat-drop. It is drawn on the chart only. It never overwrites a real transmitter trace, isn't saved to your library, and doesn't feed SAC calculations or exports.

### Clearer bulk editing of tags, dive types, buddies, and gear

Bulk-editing a reference collection across several dives used to mean Add, Remove, and Replace mode chips that hid what the selected dives already had, where "remove" meant adding an item to a removal list and "Replace" could silently wipe everything. Those are replaced by a plain three-state checklist: **checked** puts the item on every selected dive, **unchecked** takes it off every dive that has it, and a **dash** leaves the mix exactly as it is. The dash is the default, so nothing changes unless you say so, and you can see at a glance which items only some of your selected dives carry.

### Fewer false duplicates when importing files, and Consolidate when they are duplicates

Importing a file could flag a dive as a possible duplicate of one logged months earlier and kilometres away, purely because the two happened to last the same number of minutes. Time is now a necessary condition rather than one ingredient of a score: two dives whose start times differ by fifteen minutes or more can no longer be matched to each other, however alike their depth and duration.

When an imported dive genuinely *is* the same dive as one you already have, file imports now offer **Consolidate**, folding it onto the existing dive with the same merge that dive-computer downloads already had, either one card at a time or for the whole import at once. Because a file can't prove which computer recorded it, file imports never consolidate automatically; you choose. Re-importing the exact same file is now recognized as such and defaults to Skip.

### An interrupted download keeps the dives it already pulled

A download that timed out or that you cancelled used to throw away everything it had transferred. It now offers to import the dives it managed to pull, which also advances your resume point, so a long download over a slow link can be completed across several attempts instead of restarting from nothing each time.

### "Parse raw data" completes its platform coverage

The dive detail page's "Parse raw data" action, which re-runs the parser over the raw data already in your library without re-downloading from your computer, now works on Android and Linux. Those two platforms had been returning "not yet implemented" when you tapped it; it now behaves the same on all five platforms.

## 🐞 Bug fixes

### Large Shearwater logbooks can finally be downloaded

Divers with big logbooks on a Shearwater couldn't get their older dives at all. The computer delivered dives newest-first, and a large download over Bluetooth usually timed out partway; because a partial download was discarded outright, the resume point never moved, and the next attempt restarted from the beginning and timed out at the same place. Even had it not been discarded, newest-first ordering can't be resumed: keeping the part that transferred means keeping the dives you already had.

Dives now arrive oldest-first, so an interrupted download leaves a contiguous run of your oldest dives that you can import and resume from. Separately, deleting a dive *on the computer* leaves a marker in its manifest, and a buffering bug meant any still-valid dive sitting behind such a marker was silently never downloaded. Divers who had tidied up their watch simply found older dives missing, with no error, and a progress bar that never reached 100%. Both are fixed, and progress now completes. This covers the Petrel, Petrel 2 and 3, Perdix and Perdix 2, Perdix AI, Teric, Peregrine and Peregrine TX, Tern, and NERD; the older Predator is unchanged.

### Android USB and serial downloads no longer crash on release builds

Downloading from a dive computer over a USB cable crashed instantly on Android release builds, every time, before any progress appeared. Android's release optimizer was deleting the serial read and write methods that the native download code looks up by name at runtime, because nothing it could see was calling them; the lookup then failed and the process was killed. Those methods are now pinned, the native code clears the failed lookup rather than proceeding with an error pending, and a build-time check verifies the methods survive optimization. This is a different fault from the memory-reference leak and the process isolation that shipped in 1.5.9. It is the reason USB downloads crashed on every single attempt, and it fired before any of that other machinery ever ran. Bluetooth downloads were never affected.

### Built-in dive types no longer vanish after "Replace everywhere"

A "Replace everywhere" restore could leave your dive-type list empty. The restore clears every synced table and refills it from the cloud copy, but built-in reference rows are deliberately left out of that copy, so the fifteen built-in dive types were deleted and nothing put them back. Existing dives kept their assignments, but the picker and Settings → Manage → Dive Types were blank with no way to recover. The restore now spares built-in rows, and the app re-asserts them every time it opens your library, so an affected install repairs itself on the next launch with nothing for you to do and no dive losing its types.

### Android: locked out by "Database upgrade failed"

Android divers who had chosen their own backup folder could be locked out of the app after updating, facing a fatal "Database upgrade failed ... path = 'content:'" screen on every launch. Despite the message, the schema upgrade wasn't at fault. The safety backup Submersion takes just before a migration was treating the Android folder you'd picked as if it were an ordinary file path, and tried to create a directory literally named `content:` somewhere it wasn't allowed to write. That safety copy now goes to the app's own storage, which is always writable, while your chosen folder keeps receiving normal backups as before. Anyone stuck on this screen is unblocked by installing this update.

### Per-cylinder SAC returns on older dives

SAC by cylinder could be blank on dives that showed a perfectly good overall SAC. When a dive is reparsed, re-imported, or consolidated from more than one computer, its cylinders are rebuilt with new internal identities while the recorded pressure curve still refers to the old ones. The overall SAC calculation tolerated that; the per-cylinder one demanded an exact match and found nothing. The pressure data is now relinked to the right cylinder, both permanently as a one-time repair when you upgrade and on the fly as the page loads, so affected dives show their per-cylinder numbers again immediately. Manually logged dives with no profile but a recorded average depth now get per-cylinder SAC too.

### The profile chart no longer crashes on dives without a transmitter

Some dive computers record a tank-pressure channel even when no transmitter is paired, filling it with zeros. That flat series collapsed the pressure axis to zero width, and hovering or scrubbing the profile chart on such a dive crashed with a NaN error and never showed a tooltip; in fullscreen playback it cascaded into a second failure. A pressure series that carries no information is now simply not drawn, and the chart's axis mapping can no longer divide by a zero range. In a large logbook this could affect a couple of hundred dives.

### Recent dives on the home tab follows your list settings

The home screen's Recent dives ignored your dive-list display settings: it always drew the full detailed card, never the compact one, and disregarded the fields you'd assigned to each slot. It was a separate copy of the dive row that had drifted from the real one. Both lists now render through a single shared widget, so the home tab honours your view mode and your card configuration and can't drift again.

### SAC statistics from MacDive imports

After importing a MacDive database, per-dive SAC appeared but the aggregate SAC statistics (SAC Rate Trend, SAC Records, and SAC by Tank Role) all read "No data." The MacDive importer was writing cylinder volume and working pressure under the wrong names, so every imported cylinder was stored with no volume, and the statistics that need a volume matched nothing. Future MacDive database imports now populate those statistics. Dives already imported carry no cylinder volume and will need to be re-imported, or given a volume on the cylinder, to recover volume-based SAC statistics. MacDive XML imports were never affected.

### Garmin FIT dives get their names

FIT files carry no dive name, because Garmin puts it in the exported filename instead, so an imported dive arrived unnamed. Submersion now takes the name from the filename, stripping the extension and any leading dive number, so `22 Dos Ojos Cenote Dive (Barbie Line).fit` imports as "Dos Ojos Cenote Dive (Barbie Line)". A file named only with a number leaves the dive unnamed rather than titling it with a bare digit.

### Smaller fixes

"Save as Set" in the equipment editor reported success but saved a set that no diver could see, because it was stored without an owner and every list filters by the active diver; it now saves correctly, and is hidden when you're bulk-editing several dives at once. A recorded GPS track whose boat sat still could report its duration as zero minutes, because the track's end was taken from the last recorded position rather than the moment you stopped recording.

## 🔧 Under the hood

Three schema migrations ship in this release: dive plans and their cylinders and segments, the GPS track tables, and the one-time repair that relinks stranded tank-pressure data. All three run automatically on upgrade. The two dive matchers, one for downloads and one for file imports, now share a single scoring implementation instead of maintaining parallel copies, and a dead standalone import path and an orphaned review screen were removed, taking nearly four thousand lines with them. The Android instrumented test suite, which had quietly stopped compiling, builds and runs again. All new interface text is translated into all ten supported languages.
